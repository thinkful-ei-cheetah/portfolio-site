About

Me.

My full name is Michael Verdi and I’m a builder at heart and love creating software that solves real world problems. I grew up in Baltimore, but have since lived in San Francisco, Chicago, Washington DC and Singapore.

My path to programming is a bit different from your average coder. I graduated with a degree in Entrepreneurship because I believe businesses can change the world. I saw software as a means to an end, a super power for building products and services that help people.

Over the course of six months, I taught myself enough to be dangerous and got a job as a Ruby on Rails developer for (at the time) LivingSocial and then New Relic. I learned the importance of shipping code early and often, writing tests and communicating frequently with all involved stakeholders.

During my time at New Relic, an opportunity emerged for me to start my own business. Although it wasn’t in software, I took the leap and partnered with a college friend to start Cigar City CrossFit in Tampa. Over the course of three years, I built the business from $0 to $26k in average monthly revenue. Running a CrossFit gym taught me the importance of prioritization, customer service and community building.

Ultimately, I decided to sell the business in early 2019 because I love software. No longer just a means to an end, I love working in code day in and day out. Although I still strongly value delivering real business value, I find I am happiest when I see a beautifully encapsulated class with well-named methods and a test suite to boot!
